Six Months of
This.

A record of what I have been living with, Pematangsiantar.

```

This morning, I woke up and noticed my neighbour — the one known for suspicious activities — entering or leaving his house. Almost immediately, a chorus of noises began from the surrounding area, as if a group of self-proclaimed guardians were monitoring my every move.

It felt like they were deliberately creating a disturbance whenever I stirred, as though my mere presence caused them discomfort. The entire scene gave me the impression that these individuals might be working in concert with my neighbour.

✦

And then there is the neighbour himself — the one I know deals in drugs — who seems to mirror my every movement. Whenever I stir, he appears, stepping outside as if summoned by my waking. His timing is uncanny, almost deliberate, as though my life has become a silent cue for his own ritual of emergence.

Alone in the house, I began to understand why my mother used to cough so often — perhaps she, too, could sense or hear the constant, oppressive presence of these unseen watchers. Something that never seemed to happen before.

Who are these elusive figures who haunt the edges of my life? Why do they linger in my surroundings, clinging to the air like shadows that refuse to dissipate?
✦

Their presence is unbroken — a constant weight pressing against my awareness. For months, I have observed them lingering in the area, ever-present, yet when real issues arise, they remain apathetic. Unresolved and unresponsive.

Who could be so inconsiderate as to blare their horn at this hour of the night? Such reckless disruption is intolerable. As I moved, cars, trucks, and motorcycles also seemed to stir in motion, their engines revving as if in response to my presence. Who are these people behaving so recklessly and thoughtlessly?

I cannot help but wonder: are they the true source of the disturbances — the silent architects of the problems that have plagued this neighbourhood? Their movements feel deliberate, their presence heavy.

Are these merely coincidences, or am I surrounded by a web of watchers, tethered to my every breath — something far more insidious?
Filed under: neighbourhood disturbances, six months and counting
Location: Pematangsiantar, North Sumatra
Mood: observing, questioning, exhausted
```
