A Bank That Contradicts Itself: BCA's Email-Verification Bug, Documented

Bank Central Asia's app accepted a request to update my registered email, sent a verification link promising it would stay valid until June 24, 2026 — then rejected the same email as "incorrect," and called its own link expired less than an hour after sending it. Four screenshots, in chronological order.
FILED JUNE 2026 · BCA ACCOUNT HOLDER · 4 MIN READ

Summary

BCA's app confirmed an email-change request and sent a verification email to my new address. Twenty minutes later, the exact same change — same current email, same new email — returned "Incorrect current email." Minutes after that, clicking the verification link the bank had just sent returned "Change Email Failed: the link is invalid/has expired," despite the email itself stating the link would stay valid for another day. Three different parts of the same system, three different answers, inside roughly thirty minutes.

Background

My BCA account is registered to kristinasinaga.pv2@gmail.com. After regaining access to that inbox through Google's own account-recovery flow, I decided to migrate my registered email to a Proton Mail address, kristinapestariasinaga@proton.me, for better account security going forward. That required submitting a standard email-change request through the BCA mobile app — a routine task that should only need the current email, the new email, and a confirmation step.

In roughly thirty minutes, three parts of BCA's own system gave three incompatible answers to the same simple question — is kristinasinaga.pv2@gmail.com a valid, currently registered email? First it was valid enough to trigger a verification email. Then it was rejected as "incorrect." Then the verification link generated from that same valid request was declared expired, more than a day ahead of the deadline BCA's own email had stated.

Why This Matters

For an individual customer, this isn't cosmetic. Losing the ability to update a registered email can mean missing security alerts, being unable to complete identity-verification steps the bank itself requires, or getting stuck in a loop with no clear way out.

BCA is one of the largest private banks in Southeast Asia. A self-contradicting bug in basic identity verification, at that scale, is an operational and security concern — not just an inconvenience. When a system gives inconsistent answers to an identical input within the same half hour, that points to a synchronization problem between whichever services check "current email," issue verification tokens, and validate those tokens against each other.
