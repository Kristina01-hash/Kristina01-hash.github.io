Unfiltered and Unbothered 2.0: A Masterclass in Eye-Rolling and Emotional Evasion

My Official Thank-You Note to the Clown Academy Formerly Known as Society

Introduction: Grab Your Popcorn, Darlings

Welcome to the circus — hope you brought snacks, because this tragicomedy runs on an endless loop. Every morning, I wake up, stretch, and thank the cosmos that I still don’t have the energy to care about the fragile feelings of human soap bubbles. Honestly, it’s liberating. While the world trades authenticity for approval stamps, I’m over here collecting zero apologies and maximum peace. Expect truth bombs, sarcasm, and possibly a few bruised egos. Seatbelts optional, popcorn mandatory.

Act One: Auditions for the Role of “Normal”

Everyone’s out here twisting like pretzels for validation, while I’ve upgraded to the deluxe “Zero Filters, Infinite Chaos” edition of myself. I don’t bend; I snap back. You can call it arrogance — I call it efficient self-defense against mediocrity. The world wants me to smile sweetly while it self-destructs, but I’d rather narrate the disaster in real time with popcorn in one hand and a megaphone in the other.

Think I’m harsh? Darling, I’m being generous. The average human couldn’t locate a clue with a map, flashlight, and a search-and-rescue team. This isn’t arrogance — it’s observation. I’ve met reality, dated it briefly, and ghosted it after realizing it was faking enlightenment.

Act Two: Government Gong Show

Ah, the grand production — a live-action farce featuring the nation’s best and dimmest. Our beloved “protectors” roam the streets like malfunctioning GPS systems, mistaking noise for enforcement. They rev engines, trigger sirens, and call it operation excellence. Honey, if stupidity were an Olympic sport, these folks would sweep gold, silver, and whatever’s left of bronze.

Shout-out to the scriptwriters of this bureaucratic soap opera: it takes talent to hire so many incompetents and call it policy. Bravo! The plot twist? They actually believe they’re saving us. Sweet delusion is the only thing running efficiently around here.

Act Three: Neighborhood Netflix, Starring Me

Picture it: At midnight, in the early morning, or during the day, the cast of Idiots with Alarms performs their nightly, morning, or daytime overture of chaos. Meanwhile, I’m indoors, enjoying my coffee, mentally composing this love letter to dysfunction. Even my neighbors get in on the act — coughing theatrically on cue, as if auditioning for the role of “Concerned Citizen #4.” Honestly, give these people awards. They’re acting their way through incompetence like it’s performance art.

Finale: Still Unbothered, Still Fabulous

Through the drama, noise, and tragic attempts at relevance, I remain the unflappable main character. My peace is Wi-Fi–protected, my sarcasm industrial strength, and my tolerance level at minus infinity. Let the clowns perform. I’ll be over here thriving, moisturized, and mildly entertained by the spectacle.

Because the secret weapon isn’t rage — it’s indifference, wrapped in wit and sprinkled with glitter. You can waste your time trying to fix stupidity, or you can watch it self-destruct with style. I chose style. Always have. Always will.

Note: I'm the CEO, CFO, and sole shareholder of my own soul. Occasionally, someone might think they've "accessed" me while I'm snoozing—but relax, that's just their little illusion of control doing cartwheels. Their security blanket, not my problem. Their national drama, not my subplot.

I remain fabulously unfiltered, effortlessly unbothered, and mildly amused. You know what you know (shocking, I know), and you don't know what you don't know—which tracks, really. So go ahead, play detective and figure out why the nation's still stuck on level one of the Civilization game. Spoiler: it's hard to upgrade when mediocrity is everyone's favorite hobby. Meanwhile, I'm over here embracing excellence like it's a full-time romance. 💅

Mic dropped, Wi-Fi strong, soul unbothered.

So, dear society, keep your scripts, your auditions, and your misguided attempts at control. I’m here for the show, popcorn in hand, unfiltered and unbothered as ever.

With all the eye-rolls and none of the apologies,

Kristina P. Sinaga
